This summer I took advantage of an extraordinary opportunity offered by the University of Coimbra that was funded by the Erasmus programme. They organized an introductory course on spatial analysis with a focus on health applications that took place between 11th and 15th of July. I had the chance to be taught by two very good professors which made the entire experience remarkable and memorable. I learned a lot and, hopefully, more oportunities like these arise in the future.
Last day of the course was entirely dedicated to an evaluation. We made teams of three. Keep in mind that teams with two people from the same institution were not allowed and that made things a lot funnier and enjoyable. The assignment evaluation is embeded below (I hope I wont get in trouble for sharing it).


Our team chose to use the first method of the assignment. If you can not be bothered to read the document, I will make a short TLDR. We had to choose a few indicators (between 3 and 5) that had data at perish/neighborghood level for either Lisabon, Porto or Berlin that were relevant in the analysis of either air quality or heat wave impact/probability. Then we had to standardize the values of the indicators using the z-score statistical method and then add the values up in order to create an index that would show which perishes/neighborghoods need the most intervantion. In the end, we had to check if our index values were more or less clustered in certain areas. To do this, we performed some clustering analysis using the Anselin Local Moran's tool in ArcMap. Below are the results of our analysis of the heat wave impact in Porto (keep in mind we didn't even have 24 hours available to do this haha).

The NDVI values are flipped! Higher values mean less healthy vegetation and lower values mean healthier vegetation.

We chose 4 indicators: population density, average NDVI for each perish, built area (% of total) and elderly population (% of total). We also performed some cluster analysis on each of them.

This was the final result. We first mapped the index and then we performed some cluster analysis on it.
